­ЪДа Simulation Models

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

This sim utilizes a multi-layered deterministic approach to represent fire behavior:

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
    ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • Surface Fire Model: Based on the Rothermel Surface Fire Spread equations. Spread rates are modulated by fuel type (Timber, Brush, Grass) and slope.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • Wind Vectors: A dynamic vector field affects heat transfer. Fire spreads significantly faster up-wind and up-slope due to pre-heating of fuels.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • Fuel Moisture Content (FMC): A moisture-debt model. Fire cannot transition to a "Burning" state until the moisture value reaches 0 via evaporative cooling.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • Spatial Grid: A 2D Hash Grid (Spatial Partitioning) manages collision and proximity checks for 100,000+ instances at 60fps.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

­ЪЈЎ№ИЈ Visuals

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

Uses GPGPU-style Instancing for high-performance rendering. The skybox cycles through HSL color space based on the simulation time (SimMinutes), while the directional light is locked to prevent visibility loss during night cycles.

Fuel & State Information

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

The simulation tracks the moisture and combustion state of every individual fuel unit. Higher moisture requires more "Heat Units" from nearby fires to evaporate before ignition occurs.

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

­ЪЪб Vegetation Colors (States)

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
Green (Live): High moisture. Fire-resistant until dried out.
┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
Gold (Drying): Moisture below threshold. Ignition is imminent.
┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
Orange (Burning): Currently consuming fuel and emitting heat.
┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
Black (Charred): Dead fuel. Cannot be re-ignited.
┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а

­ЪЊЅ Moisture Thresholds

┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
    ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • 5% РђЊ 12%: "Drought" conditions. Rapid spread and high intensity.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • 15% РђЊ 25%: Standard conditions. Fire spreads but can be managed by breaks.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
  • 30%+: "Point of Extinction." Fire struggles to sustain itself.
    • ┬а ┬а ┬а ┬а
