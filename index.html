Fuel Moisture Physics

1. The "Boiling Point" Delay

In reality, fire cannot consume fuel until the water inside it has evaporated.

  • Higher Moisture: The fire must "cook" the tree longer before it actually catches fire. This creates a buffer where you see the trees turn yellow/brown but not yet red.
  • Lower Moisture: This stage is skipped almost instantly. The fire moves with "flashy" behavior, typical of a drought-stricken forest.

2. The Ignition Threshold

This simulator uses logic based on drying states:

  • Live to Drying: Occurs when moisture drops below 5x the base moisture setting.
  • Drying to Burning: Occurs when moisture hits 0.
  • Insight: At 30%, the forest is roughly 3x harder to ignite than at 10% because heat must work longer to "drain" the water units.

4. Wind Interaction

  • Low Moisture + High Wind: Firestorm scenario. Rapid forward drying ahead of the flame front.
  • High Moisture + High Wind: The "Protective" effect. Wind can blow heat away before it evaporates water in the next fuel source.
